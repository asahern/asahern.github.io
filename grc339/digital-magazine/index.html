Winery Traffic Deterred by Heat

Wineries across Tin City have been experiencing significant revenue loss due to the rising temperatures this Summer. Paso Robles is expected to hit over 100º this weekend, and tasting rooms are anticipating low foot traffic. Tin City, a business district in Paso Robles, California, is home to over two dozen wineries. The region requently experiences over 100º heat waves that have had detrimental affects on the industry, from tourism to crop harvesting.

Tin City Wins Big in California State Fair Wine Competition

Tin City is proud to announce that 9 of our producers took home major awards on their wines at the California State Fair Wine Competition that occured last week. These awards include Best Red Wine, Best New Micro Winery, Most Creative Blend, Best Wine and Cheese Experience, Best Virtual Experience, and more!

Tin City Weekly Update

Rising temperatures and changes in tourism have impacted Tin City's foot traffic all year, with a slight decline becoming apparent as the SUmmer continues. Changes to COVID-19 laws as well as higher gas prices may have an impact on this.

Shipping Delays Across California

Several wineries have been experiencing shipping delays from various shipping companies including FedEx, GSO, and UPS. Weather and extreme heat have caused delayes all over the west, and supply chain issues continue to impact shipping throughout the entire country.

Etto Discount for Tin City Employees

Etto Pastifico announced this week that its new discount for Tin City employees is buy on get one free on all fresh pasta, sauce, and sausages.

Gas Leak Reports

Several reports of small gas leaks have been made from producers in central Tin City. So far Six Test Kitchen, Etto, Barrelhouse, and Jacob Toft have reported minor gas leaks but all issues have since been fixed with to lasting repercussions.
